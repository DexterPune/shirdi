Top Places to Visit in Shirdi and Nearby with a Mini Bus

1. Shirdi Sai Baba Temple:

The main attraction in Shirdi, this temple is dedicated to Sai Baba and attracts millions of devotees each year. The temple complex includes various sacred sites associated with the saint’s life.

2. Dwarkamai Masjid:

Located near the Sai Baba Temple, Dwarkamai is a sacred mosque where Sai Baba spent a significant portion of his life. It is a place of spiritual significance and pilgrimage for devotees.

3. Chavadi:

Another important site near the Sai Baba Temple, Chavadi is where Sai Baba used to sleep every alternate night. The building is now a place of worship and holds special significance for followers.

4. Shani Shingnapur:

Located about 70 kilometers from Shirdi, this village is famous for its temple dedicated to Lord Shani, the Hindu god associated with the planet Saturn. It’s known for its unique tradition of houses without doors, reflecting the villagers' belief in Shani's protection.

5. Samadhi Mandir:

The Samadhi Mandir is where Sai Baba's mortal remains were laid to rest. It is a significant place of worship and pilgrimage, drawing devotees from all over the world.

6. Khandoba Temple:

Situated near the Shirdi bus stand, Khandoba Temple is another important spiritual site. It is dedicated to Khandoba, a form of Lord Shiva, and is where Sai Baba was first recognized as a spiritual guru.

7. Gurusthan:

This is the spot where Sai Baba was first spotted as a young boy sitting under a neem tree. Gurusthan is a revered site where devotees come to offer their prayers.

8. Lendi Baug:

A beautiful garden in Shirdi, Lendi Baug was frequented by Sai Baba for daily walks and meditation. The garden is well-maintained and offers a serene environment for visitors.

9. Wet N Joy Water Park:

Located close to the Sai Baba Temple, this water park offers fun and entertainment for families and groups. It’s a great spot to relax and enjoy some leisure time during your visit to Shirdi.

10. Ajanta and Ellora Caves:

Although a bit further away (approximately 100-120 kilometers from Shirdi), these UNESCO World Heritage sites are worth visiting for their ancient rock-cut caves, temples, and monasteries. The caves feature intricate carvings and stunning architecture, representing the rich history of India.

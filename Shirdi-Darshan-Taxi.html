Top 10 Places to Visit in Shirdi and Nearby

1. Sai Baba Temple:

The main attraction in Shirdi, this temple is the final resting place of Sai Baba and attracts millions of devotees each year. It serves as the heart of the spiritual journey for many, providing a place for worship, reflection, and devotion.

2. Dwarkamai:

A significant site where Sai Baba spent a considerable part of his life, Dwarkamai is located within the temple complex. This mosque is revered as a place where Sai Baba performed numerous miracles and engaged with his followers, making it a crucial stop for those seeking to understand his teachings and legacy.

3. Chavadi:

Chavadi is a historical place associated with Sai Baba, where he used to sleep every alternate night. This site holds great significance for devotees, as it symbolizes the simple yet profound lifestyle of Sai Baba, reflecting his humility and connection with the community.

4. Lendi Baug:

Lendi Baug is a serene garden where Sai Baba used to meditate and water the plants. It’s a peaceful spot for reflection, offering visitors a tranquil environment to connect with nature and contemplate their spiritual journeys.

5. Sai Heritage Village:

A theme park that offers a glimpse into the life and times of Sai Baba through various exhibits and models. It provides a unique opportunity for visitors to engage with the stories and teachings of Sai Baba in a family-friendly setting.

6. Shani Shingnapur:

Located about 70 kilometers from Shirdi, Shani Shingnapur is famous for its temple dedicated to Lord Shani. The village is unique for its tradition of having no doors in the houses, symbolizing the belief that the deity protects the residents from harm, making it an intriguing visit for travelers.

7. Wet N Joy Water Park:

A fun destination for families, this water park offers a range of rides and attractions, making it a great place to unwind after your Darshan. It is an ideal spot for those looking to relax and enjoy some recreational activities in a vibrant atmosphere.

8. Khandoba Temple:

A small temple dedicated to Lord Khandoba, which is of historical significance as it is where Sai Baba first stayed when he arrived in Shirdi. The temple reflects the region's rich cultural heritage and provides insights into the local traditions.

9. Upasani Maharaj Ashram:

Located a few kilometers from Shirdi, this ashram is dedicated to one of Sai Baba's prominent disciples, Upasani Maharaj. The ashram is a serene place for spiritual seekers and offers teachings that continue to inspire many followers of Sai Baba.

10. Gurusthan:

The place where Sai Baba was first spotted meditating under a neem tree when he came to Shirdi as a young boy. Gurusthan holds immense spiritual significance for devotees, symbolizing the beginning of Sai Baba's divine journey and his connection to Shirdi.

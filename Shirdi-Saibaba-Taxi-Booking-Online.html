Top Places to Visit in Shirdi with Shirdi Saibaba Travels

1. Shirdi Sai Baba Temple:

The epicenter of devotion in Shirdi, the Sai Baba Temple, is where devotees offer prayers, participate in aarti, and seek blessings. The temple complex also houses several other shrines dedicated to various deities, creating a spiritual atmosphere that resonates with peace and devotion.

2. Dwarkamai:

Located adjacent to the temple, Dwarkamai is the mosque where Sai Baba spent a significant part of his life. It is a place of deep spiritual significance, with the sacred dhuni (fire) that Baba lit still burning today, symbolizing the eternal flame of faith and devotion.

3. Chavadi:

Chavadi was where Sai Baba spent every alternate night during the last decade of his life. It’s an important place for devotees, symbolizing Baba’s care and protection, and serves as a reminder of his humility and connection with his followers.

4. Gurusthan:

The place where Sai Baba was first spotted meditating under a neem tree. A small shrine now marks this spot, and devotees believe that the leaves of this tree have healing powers, making it a site of reverence and spiritual healing.

5. Shani Shingnapur:

Located around 70 km from Shirdi, this village is famous for its Shani temple and the unique tradition of having no doors in houses, trusting Lord Shani to protect them. This belief showcases the deep faith of the villagers and attracts many visitors seeking blessings.

6. Lendi Baug:

A beautiful garden where Sai Baba used to meditate and water the plants. The garden features a lamp that Baba lit, which still burns today, symbolizing his eternal presence and love for nature, providing a tranquil space for visitors.

7. Khandoba Mandir:

This temple is where Sai Baba was first recognized as a holy person. It’s a significant site for devotees and holds deep historical importance, serving as a reminder of Baba's divine journey and the impact he had on those around him.

8. Sai Heritage Village:

An interactive museum showcasing the life and times of Sai Baba through statues, sculptures, and replicas. It offers a glimpse into the era when Sai Baba lived, allowing visitors to connect with his legacy and teachings in a profound way.

9. Maruti Mandir:

Dedicated to Lord Hanuman, this temple is one of the oldest in Shirdi and is visited by those who wish to seek blessings for strength and courage. The temple's vibrant atmosphere and historical significance make it a popular stop for pilgrims.

10. Dixit Wada Museum:

Located within the temple complex, this museum displays artifacts and belongings of Sai Baba, including his clothes, footwear, and cooking utensils, offering a personal connection to the saint and allowing visitors to experience a slice of his daily life.
